I am a senior in the School of Communication at Virginia Tech, concentrating in
strategic communication and digital media. This portfolio documents my work in CMST
4714: The Human Communicator in an Era of AI, where I have spent a semester building
the theoretical vocabulary to answer a question I walked in with: what does a human
communicator actually do that an AI cannot?

My short answer, after a semester of sitting with Shannon and Weaver, Escarpit, Hall,
and Eco: a human communicator does not just transmit information. She produces meaning
in a specific social and historical context, for a specific reader, in full awareness
that her words will be interpreted, reinterpreted, and — now — processed by systems
that have their own intentions about what content should reach which audience.

That last part is what this course calls *intentio algorithmi*. Understanding it does
not make me pessimistic about my field. It makes me clearer about what I am actually
being hired to do.

## What I bring

Before I arrived in CMST 4714, I spent two years as a writer and content coordinator
for a regional nonprofit focused on housing equity. That work taught me that writing
for a specific community is fundamentally different from writing for a general audience
— that clarity and trust are not the same thing, and that you cannot optimize your way
to either. I also interned with a mid-size marketing agency where I watched a content
team adopt AI drafting tools and spend more time editing for tone than they had
previously spent writing. I have a theory about why that happened, and The Paper is
where I work it out.
