**Assignment:** AI Analysis Memo 3 — Broken Artifact Diagnosis
**Submitted:** November 4, 2026
**Audience:** Communications Director, regional transit authority

---

**TO:** Communications Director
**FROM:** Robin Hokie
**DATE:** November 4, 2026
**RE:** AI-generated service alert — encoding failure diagnosis

On October 17, your agency's automated service alert system sent riders the following
message via SMS and app notification:

> "Service on Route 12 will be suspended for scheduled maintenance from 6:00 AM to
> 2:00 PM on Saturday. Riders are encouraged to plan accordingly."

Fourteen riders who relied on Route 12 for Saturday morning medical appointments missed
those appointments. Three filed formal complaints. The root failure was not the
suspension itself — it was the message.

**The diagnosis:**

Using Hall's encoding/decoding model: the message was encoded for an imagined audience
of flexible commuters with alternative transportation options. The agency's AI drafting
tool drew from a template pool calibrated to service alerts that had not generated
complaints — a population that almost certainly overrepresents riders who have cars.
The preferred reading ("plan accordingly") assumes the reader has options to plan with.
The actual audience included riders for whom Route 12 is the only option, who decoded
the message as either low-stakes (it did not name the stakes) or unanswerable (no
alternative service was offered).

The *intentio algorithmi* at work: the drafting tool optimized for messages that do not
generate complaints, not for messages that serve all affected riders. That is not the
same objective, and in a transit equity context, it is an ethical problem.

**Recommendation:**

Require any AI-drafted service alert affecting riders who may lack alternatives to pass
a plain-language equity review before sending. Specifically: add a field in the
drafting workflow that asks "does this service connect any riders with no alternative?"
If yes, the alert must name the affected population, the impact, and the agency's
response. No AI tool should clear that field automatically.

---

**Reflection** *(added for portfolio)*

What I learned from writing this memo: the broken artifact is always downstream of a
broken intention somewhere in the production chain. The tool did not fail — it succeeded
at the wrong task. That is an *intentio algorithmi* problem, not a technology problem,
and it is not fixable by getting a better tool.
